Why this number is likely understated
The 31,636t figure uses a sector-average EEIO factor. In construction, embodied carbon in materials and supply chain typically represents 80%+ of actual footprint. CBRE's own Scope 3 is not covered by their SBTi — the real number could be substantially higher.
The Scope 3 gap is the priority
CBRE's SBTi covers Scope 1 and 2 only. For a construction category manager, this means the materials supply chain — concrete, steel, glass — is entirely outside the target framework. That is where the commercial leverage should focus.
A supplier-specific factor changes the picture
CBRE's operational emissions (Scope 1+2) are strong and declining. If they provide a supplier-specific intensity factor anchored to verified operational data, your Category 1 attribution likely falls. The bigger prize is pushing Scope 3 coverage into the SBTi.
