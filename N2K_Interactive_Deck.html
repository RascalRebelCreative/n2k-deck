Why the gap exists
Business travel frequency
Air travel is typically the largest single carbon driver in professional services — often 60–80% of total Scope 1+2. Remote-first policy is the most powerful lever.
Office energy source
Suppliers with 100% renewable office energy (REGO-verified) show 15–25% lower intensity. Easily achievable — low cost, widely available.
Remote working policy
Shifting from opt-in remote to opt-in on-site materially changes emissions. The default matters as much as the policy ceiling.
Data centre intensity
Tech-heavy professional services firms with significant cloud/data infrastructure see this as a growing Scope 3 exposure. Relevant for IT consulting.

Data sources used: existing spend analytics, CDP supply chain pull (2024), SBTi targets database, TPI assessments. No new supplier questionnaires required.

Pathway decision — which lever closes the gap?

Discover
Know your supply base — who is ready, who is at risk, and why. Not a league table — a diagnostic.
NTS component breakdown

The NTS scores supplier transition readiness — not current performance. A supplier with high ambition and low progress is a different commercial problem to one with no targets at all. The component breakdown is the diagnosis that determines which lever to apply.

Priority Score = NTS × log(emissions volume)

You cannot engage every supplier. The Priority Score surfaces where category manager time has the highest combined commercial and carbon leverage. Log transformation prevents a single large supplier from collapsing the ranking.

Priority #1 is Meridian Consulting — not the largest supplier (Apex) and not the worst NTS (Legacy). It is the combination of meaningful emissions volume and a NTS gap that is commercially addressable. That is where effort has the highest leverage.

Substitution matrix — readiness vs procurement influence
Decision framework

Substitution pressure changes in-life dynamics. A supplier who knows alternatives exist and are being benchmarked engages differently with performance conversations than one who assumes incumbency is guaranteed.

Impact × proximity — lever selection
Applied to professional services

Lever mismatch is the most common failure mode in procurement sustainability. High impact, low proximity problems are not solved by KPIs alone. The model prevents applying the wrong tool.
